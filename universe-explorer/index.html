Chapter 02 — Galaxies

Spiral
Galaxy

A galaxy is a gravitationally bound system of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter. The Milky Way contains 200–400 billion stars arranged in elegant spiral arms.

There are over 2 trillion galaxies in the observable universe. They come in spirals, ellipticals, and irregular shapes — cosmic cities of stars.

  • Milky Way: 100,000 light-years across, ~200B stars
  • Andromeda: Our nearest large neighbor, 2.5M light-years away
  • Dark Matter: 85% of galactic mass is invisible

Chapter 03 — Solar System

Our Cosmic
Neighborhood

A central star with planets orbiting in near-perfect ellipses. Our solar system formed 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing cloud of hydrogen.

From rocky inner worlds to gas giants with rings, moons, and asteroid belts — each orbit tells a story of gravitational balance.

  • Sun: Contains 99.86% of the system's mass
  • Orbital speeds: Earth moves at 107,000 km/h
  • Scale: Light takes 5.5 hours to cross the system

Chapter 04 — Time

The Fabric of
Time

Time is not a universal constant. It bends, stretches, and flows at different rates depending on speed and gravity. Einstein wove time together with space into a single fabric: spacetime.

Near massive objects, clocks tick slower. At near-light speeds, time dilates. GPS satellites must correct for relativity every single day.

  • Planck Time: 5.39 × 10-44 s — smallest meaningful unit
  • Arrow of Time: Entropy always increases, giving time direction
  • At 99.9% c: 1 ship-year = 22 Earth-years

Chapter 05 — Relativity

Curved
Spacetime

Gravity is not a force — it's the curvature of spacetime caused by mass. Objects follow curved paths through this warped geometry, which we perceive as gravitational attraction.

Gμν + Λgμν = (8πG/c4) Tμν

Einstein's field equations describe how matter tells spacetime how to curve, and curved spacetime tells matter how to move.

E = mc²

Chapter 06 — Wormholes

Tunnels Through
Spacetime

Einstein-Rosen bridges could theoretically connect distant points in spacetime, creating shortcuts across millions of light-years.

An Alcubierre warp drive compresses space ahead and expands it behind, moving a bubble of flat spacetime faster than light — without violating relativity.

  • Chemical rocket to Andromeda: ~75 billion years
  • At light speed: 2.5 million years
  • Wormhole: Potentially instant

Chapter 07 — Black Holes

Event
Horizon

When matter is compressed beyond a critical density, spacetime curves so extremely that nothing — not even light — can escape. The boundary is the event horizon.

At the center lies a singularity: a point of theoretically infinite density where the known laws of physics break down entirely.

  • Sagittarius A*: 4 million solar masses at the Milky Way's center
  • M87*: First photographed black hole, 6.5 billion solar masses
  • Hawking radiation: Black holes slowly evaporate over eons

Chapter 08 — Beyond

What Lies
Beyond

Our universe may be one of infinitely many. String theory suggests up to 10500 possible universes, each with different physical constants.

In ~10100 years, the universe may reach maximum entropy — heat death. No energy gradients, no processes, no life. The ultimate fate of everything.

  • Multiverse: Infinite parallel realities with different physics
  • Quantum Gravity: The missing theory unifying GR and QM
  • Simulation?: Statistically, we may be inside one
